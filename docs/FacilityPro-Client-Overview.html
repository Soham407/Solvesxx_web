How Does a Typical Order Work?

Workflow 1: Ordering Materials

Buyer needs cleaning supplies, stationery, pest control chemicals, etc.

BuyerPlaces Order
→
AdminReviews & Approves
→
AdminCreates Purchase Order
→
SupplierAccepts & Ships
→
StorekeeperChecks Quality
→
BuyerPays & Reviews

Step by step

  • Step 1: Buyer logs in and submits an order request (e.g., "50 bottles of floor cleaner")
  • Step 2: Admin gets notified, reviews the request, and either accepts, holds, or rejects it
  • Step 3: Admin creates an internal demand (called an "indent") and sends it to the best matching supplier
  • Step 4: Supplier reviews and accepts the indent. Admin issues a formal Purchase Order
  • Step 5: Supplier ships the goods and updates the status to "Dispatched"
  • Step 6: Storekeeper inspects delivery — checks quantity (did we get all 50?) and quality (any damaged bottles?)
  • Step 7: If items fail inspection, a Return Ticket is created and sent back to supplier
  • Step 8: Supplier submits their bill. Admin verifies and pays
  • Step 9: Admin generates an invoice for the buyer. Buyer pays and leaves feedback
Key point: Every step is tracked automatically. No one needs to call or email to ask "Where is my order?" — they can just check the system.
👮

Workflow 2: Deploying Staff (Security / Housekeeping)

Buyer needs 5 security guards deployed to their building for 6 months

BuyerRequests 5 Guards
→
AdminFinds Vendor
→
SupplierSends Guards
→
SupervisorVerifies on Site
→
BuyerPays & Reviews

Step by step

  • Step 1: Buyer selects "Security Services" → picks Grade A guards → 5 people → morning shift → 6 months
  • Step 2: System automatically pulls the rate from the price list. Admin verifies and creates a service indent
  • Step 3: Admin matches to a vendor who provides Grade A guards and sends them the indent
  • Step 4: Supplier accepts and sends back a Delivery Note listing the 5 guards' names and credentials
  • Step 5: Supplier marks "Personnel Dispatched" — buyer and admin are notified
  • Step 6: Site Supervisor meets the guards at the client's location, checks that all 5 showed up and their grade matches
  • Step 7: Supervisor confirms deployment. Supplier submits their bill. Admin reconciles and invoices the buyer
  • Step 8: Buyer pays and rates the service quality

Workflow 3: Visitor Enters a Society

Someone comes to visit a resident — how the guard handles it

VisitorArrives at Gate
→
GuardRegisters Visitor
→
SystemSends SMS + Photo
→
ResidentApproves / Denies
→
GuardAllows / Blocks

Step by step

  • Step 1: Visitor arrives at the gate
  • Step 2: Guard opens the app, looks up the flat number, and registers the visitor (name, photo, phone, vehicle)
  • Step 3: System instantly sends SMS and push notification to the resident — with the visitor's photo
  • Step 4: Resident taps "Approve" or "Deny" on their phone
  • Step 5: Guard sees the response and either lets the visitor in or turns them away
For regular visitors (maids, drivers, milkmen) — they go into a separate "daily visitor" database so guards don't need to register them every day.

Workflow 4: AC Repair / Pest Control Job

Resident complains "AC not cooling" — how it gets fixed

Resident/ManagerRaises Complaint
→
AdminAssigns Technician
→
TechnicianArrives + Before Photo
→
TechnicianFixes + After Photo
→
ManagerReviews & Closes

Step by step

  • Step 1: Resident or society manager logs a complaint (e.g., "AC not cooling in Flat 302")
  • Step 2: Admin assigns an AC technician to the job
  • Step 3: Technician gets notified, arrives at location, and checks in via GPS
  • Step 4: Takes a "before" photo of the problem
  • Step 5: Does the repair — parts used are automatically logged and deducted from inventory
  • Step 6: Takes an "after" photo and marks the job as complete
  • Step 7: Manager reviews the work and closes the ticket
For pest control: Same workflow, but technicians must also fill a mandatory safety checklist (PPE) before starting, and residents get an automatic warning notification.

Workflow 5: Emergency / Panic Alert

Guard spots a fire, break-in, or medical emergency

GuardHits SOS Button
→
SystemCaptures GPS
→
SystemSends Alerts (SMS + Push)
→
Supervisor + ManagerRespond

What happens

  • Guard presses the big red SOS button on their phone
  • System instantly captures their exact GPS location
  • All supervisors and the society manager receive push notifications AND SMS simultaneously
  • Manager can see the guard's location on a map and coordinate response
  • After the situation is handled, manager adds resolution notes to close the alert
Response time: The alert reaches all relevant people within seconds — no phone calls needed.
